Since switching from digital storytelling to Computer Science and finally to Information Technology I have found my place. Starting out in digital story telling I knew right after the first class that I wanted to be more involved with math and coding. So, I switched to computer science. I enjoyed these classes but covid had just hit. Keeping up with the required schoolwork seemed impossible. As I tried to integrate a new way of living with my studies, I found that Information technology was a better fit for me. The two were very similar, sharing many cross- listed courses. The biggest difference for me was the teachers, in my computer science classes there was so much stress and pressure put on tiny assignments. I felt like I was in over my head, until I switched to Information technology. We were learning the same things, but everything seemed to make sense and the teachers were more than willing to help.

The classes are fast paced, and jam packed with useful skills. I have experience in using html, css, Java, JavaScript, Python, PHP, C#, C, C++, and swift. I know how to query a database and set one up. I’m familiar with mongoDB, mySQL, and MariaDB. I can design a website and code it from top to bottom. I have a basic understanding of how to create IOS applications in Xcode. I’ve used GitHub to work collaboratively. I am comfortable using Windows or macOS file systems. I have also used many adobe applications such as After Effects, Photoshop, Audition, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro. These examples are just scratching the surface of everything I got to experience working with throughout the program.

Over the semesters I have been lucky enough to gain knowledge in many different subjects in addition to Information Technology. Getting a certification in Cyber Security as well as a minor in art. I wanted to get the most out of my college education and believe that adding these two would only enhance my chances of receiving a job right after graduation. Art has always been a passion of mine. Adding my art minor allowed me to get experience in using online software that may be necessary for a tech job. I also received a ton of insight on layout, color schemes, and a plethora of other design principles. These skills give me an extra edge while creating apps and websites. Cyber Security is a no brainer. I have worked with Wireshark, password cracking tools, virtual environments, VMWare, IP addresses, the command line, Maltego, Docker, AWS and so much more.

Most of the time in this major I was forced to go outside of my comfort zone. Typically, I am one of a couple females, if not the only woman in my classes. I am always up for a challenge and enjoy working my hardest to represent women in STEM. I have done so by out doing myself time and time again and making the dean’s list. The engineering school at Mizzou has thoroughly prepared me for what’s to come. I am excited to graduate and see where my next journey will begin.
